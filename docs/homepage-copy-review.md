## Section 8 — Explanatory / Philosophy

*Text-focused section. No images except a before/after transformation pair below the text.*

**Headline:**
Landmarks, not lookalikes.

**Body:**
A landmark is the building people use to give directions. "Turn left at the place with the mural." "Park across from the one with the hand-painted sign." It is the building people photograph without being asked, the one they walk into because the outside told them something worth hearing.

A lookalike is everything else. The vinyl banner that could be on any storefront in any city. The template mural that looks like the one in Denver, the one in Austin, and the one in Brooklyn. The blank wall that says nothing about the people behind it. Lookalikes blend in. They get walked past. They disappear.

We paint landmarks. Murals built from real moments. Signs painted by hand with real intention. Work that belongs to your building, your block, and your story. It could not be copied onto anyone else's.

*(Last paragraph rendered in brighter white for emphasis.)*

## Notes for the Reviewer

A few things that will help:

**Voice from the Brand Book:**
Warm but direct. Professional artist. Not corporate, not hustle-culture. Show the craft, name the technique, be specific about materials and process. Customer is always the hero. Spencer/UMM is always the guide.

**Copy rules:**
- No em dashes (use periods, colons, commas instead)
- Never use: synergy, leverage, journey, seamless, transform, disrupt
- Lead with the customer's problem, not Spencer's credentials

**What "humanize" probably means here:**
The copy reads accurately but some of it is dry and constructed. The Stakes section and Process section in particular could use more warmth — they explain what happens but don't quite sound like a person talking.
