The question underneath "how much does a mural cost" is almost never really about the number. It is about not wanting to be taken advantage of, not wanting to overpay, and not being sure whether the work you are imagining is even in the range of what you can afford. That is a reasonable place to be. Here is a direct answer.

## The short version

A hand-painted mural in Richmond, Virginia typically costs between $18 and $110 per square foot, depending on complexity. A straightforward geometric or lettering mural on a prepared wall runs $18-28 per square foot. A photorealistic archival piece runs $70-110 per square foot. Most commercial murals land between $28 and $45.

For a 10x20 foot wall (200 square feet):
- Simple: $3,600-$5,600
- Moderate detail: $5,600-$9,000
- Highly detailed: $9,000-$14,000
- Photorealistic/archival: $14,000-$22,000

These ranges assume a prepared interior surface. Exterior walls, difficult surfaces, and access requirements (scaffolding, lift rental) add to that base.

## What drives the price

**Size.** Every mural is priced by square foot, not by the hour or by the day. Larger walls cost more. This is the biggest driver.

**Complexity.** A single-color geometric design takes a fraction of the time and materials of a photorealistic portrait. More colors, more detail, more reference gathering, and longer execution all push the price up. This is the second-biggest driver.

**Surface condition.** A smooth, properly primed interior wall is the baseline. Exterior brick adds roughly 15 percent. Rough or deteriorating surfaces that need significant prep add 15-25 percent or more before the design work starts. Surface preparation is not optional for work meant to last.

**Interior vs exterior.** Outdoor murals require different paints, UV-protective sealants, and in many cases anti-graffiti coating. These materials cost more. Weather conditions also affect production timelines. Interior murals are controlled environments with lower material cost and no weather variables.

**Access requirements.** Ground-level exterior walls are straightforward. A mural that requires a 20-foot lift rental or scaffolding adds real cost. For large-scale exterior work on multi-story buildings, equipment rental can add $800-$3,000 or more to the total.

**Design process.** Every project includes a site visit, a research phase, and a photorealistic 3D render showing the design on your actual wall before any painting starts. That render is included. It is not optional, because it is how you know what you are getting before any paint touches your wall. The design phase is part of the price.

## What is included

The base price includes the discovery session, site visit, reference photography, photorealistic 3D render for approval, all paints and materials, execution, and a UV-protective clear coat. It does not include scaffolding or lift rental if required, permits for large public art installations, or surface restoration work on significantly damaged walls.

## What the cost question is really asking

Cost-per-square-foot matters. What matters more is what that wall is doing right now.

A blank wall signals vacancy. A generic mural signals autopilot. A wall that has something specific to say about the building it is on, the neighborhood it belongs to, or the business it represents: that is a different kind of investment. One that people stop to photograph. One that becomes the reason people give directions using your building as the landmark.
